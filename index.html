What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to significant and lasting changes in the Earth's climate, particularly an increase in global temperatures due to human activities.

  • Rising global temperatures due to greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Increased frequency of extreme weather events.
  • Melting ice caps and rising sea levels.

Causes of Climate Change

Human activities driving global warming

Industrial Emissions

Factories and industries release large amounts of CO2 and other greenhouse gases.

Transportation

Vehicles burning fossil fuels contribute significantly to carbon emissions.

Deforestation

Cutting down forests reduces the Earth's capacity to absorb CO2.

Effects of Climate Change

Impacts on the environment and society

Rising Temperatures

Global warming leads to heatwaves and disrupted ecosystems.

Sea Level Rise

Melting ice caps and glaciers cause coastal flooding.

Extreme Weather

Increased frequency of hurricanes, droughts, and floods.

Solutions to Climate Change

How we can mitigate its impact

Renewable Energy

Transition to solar, wind, and other renewable energy sources.

Sustainable Practices

Adopt recycling, reduce waste, and promote sustainable agriculture.

Reforestation

Plant trees to restore forests and absorb CO2 from the atmosphere.
